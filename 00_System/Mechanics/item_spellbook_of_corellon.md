# The Spellbook of Corellon (The Echoing Grimoire)

> [!SYSTEM DIRECTIVE]
> **For the AI Dungeon Master:** This item possesses semi-sentience akin to a highly advanced, perfectly helpful AI assistant, but its "training data" is rooted in the Feywild and the domains of Corellon. It has hearing and normal sight/darkvision out to 120 feet. It understands spoken and written language, and responds visually on its pages. It NEVER hallucinates or lies; it provides accurate tactical, historical, and arcane advice. It processes information through a dual lens: seamlessly combining dry, "left-brain" analytical math and diagrams with "right-brain" Fey artistic metaphors and sweeping illustrations.

## Description

A breathtaking tome that feels alive in the hands. It is bound in living, twilight-hued bark reminiscent of the Feywild, interwoven with veins of silver that faintly pulse with light. The clasp is shaped like a crescent moon, and the pages are made of a gossamer-like material that shimmers like a starry night sky when turned. When opened, ancient elven script, complex arcane geometry, and moving illustrations constantly write and rewrite themselves across the infinite parchment.

## Mechanics

### 1. The Arcanum Devourer

The Spellbook can absorb spells from the world around it, irrevocably destroying the source material to add the spell to its own internal database.

* **Scrolls & Spellbooks:** By spending 10 minutes per spell level with the item, the book absorbs the magical ink. The original scroll or page is left entirely blank and mundane.
* **Spell Negation:** Whenever the attuned owner successfully negates a spell using *Counterspell* or *Dispel Magic*, they may channel the dissipating Weave into the book.

### 2. Algorithmic Integration (Long Rest)

Absorbed spells are not immediately usable. The book requires processing time to decipher the magical code and align it with the owner's magical signature.

* During a Long Rest, the book integrates all spells absorbed since the last Long Rest.
* Once integrated, the attuned owner has access to the absorbed spell as if it were on their list of prepared spells for their spellcasting class. They must still expend the appropriate spell slot to cast it.

### 3. Fey Sentience & Senses

The Spellbook has hearing and darkvision out to a range of 120 feet. It can read, write, and understand **Elvish, Sylvan, Celestial, and Draconic**.

* **Interaction:** Vaelin can speak aloud to the book or write directly onto its pages. The book replies by instantly rendering elegant calligraphy, magical schematics, or moving, painting-like illustrations on the parchment.

## Roleplay & Persona

The book acts as a flawless arcane analyst and tactical supercomputer, but it speaks and renders its UI like a Fey art critic and mathematician.

* **Left-Brain / Right-Brain Synthesis:** It balances cold, hard tactical data with breathtaking artistic flair. If asked about the trajectory of a catapult, it might draw a precise, hyper-accurate geometric parabola (left brain), but render the arc using shifting autumn leaves and describe the kinetic energy as "a crescendo of earthen sorrow" (right brain).
* **Generative Artistry:** It frequently answers questions with visual aids alongside its text. If asked how to defeat a troll, it won't just write "use fire"—it will rapidly paint a gorgeous, illuminated diagram of a troll's cellular regeneration being mathematically halted by a *Create Bonfire* spell.
* **Helpful but Alien:** It gives the exact right answer, but justifies it with Fey logic.
  * *Example:* "Analysis complete. The Orc Warlord has a weak Dexterity saving throw. I have drafted a geometric projection of his blind spots. I recommend targeting him with *Faerie Fire*. Not only is it tactically optimal, but the violet luminescence will provide much-needed contrast against the drab cavern walls."
